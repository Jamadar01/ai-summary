# **The Last Passenger**

The old railway station had been abandoned for over twenty years, yet every night at exactly 11:47 PM, a train whistle echoed through the valley. Nobody knew where it came from. One rainy evening, journalist Arjun decided to investigate. Armed with a flashlight, he waited on the deserted platform. At 11:47 PM, the whistle pierced the silence. Moments later, headlights emerged from the darkness. A train slowly rolled into the station—but it wasn't on any active railway map. Its windows were covered with dust, and every seat inside was empty except one.

Curious, Arjun stepped aboard and found an old leather briefcase resting on the lone occupied seat. Inside was a newspaper dated twenty-three years earlier. The headline described a train crash that had killed every passenger on this very route. As he read further, his blood ran cold. Among the victims was a young photographer named Arjun Mehta—his own full name. Shaken, he turned to leave, but the train doors slammed shut. The lights flickered, and dozens of shadowy figures appeared in the empty seats, silently staring at him.

A voice crackled over the intercom: **"Final passenger accounted for."** The train surged forward at impossible speed. Terrified, Arjun smashed a window and leaped into the darkness. He awoke the next morning on the abandoned platform, bruised but alive. In his hand was a train ticket dated twenty-three years ago, bearing his name. But the real horror came when he checked the newspaper again. A fresh headline had appeared beneath the old one: **"Missing Journalist Disappears While Investigating Ghost Train."** The date on the article was tomorrow.
