### The Last Lantern

In a small village surrounded by mountains, there was an old lantern hanging outside a forgotten cabin. Every night, while all other lights went out, this lantern continued to glow.

The villagers believed it was magic.

One day, a curious boy named Arif decided to discover its secret. He climbed the hill to the cabin and found an elderly woman living alone.

"How does your lantern never go out?" he asked.

The woman smiled and handed him a small box. Inside were dozens of notes.

Each note contained a kind act:

* "Helped a traveler find his way."
* "Shared food with a hungry family."
* "Taught a child to read."
* "Forgave an old friend."

"The lantern burns with these," she said.

Arif looked confused. "But these are just pieces of paper."

"They are reminders," the woman replied. "Every time we do something good, we create a little light. Most people forget about it. I choose to remember."

Years passed, and the woman grew old. Before she died, she gave the lantern to Arif.

The villagers expected the light to fade, but it shone brighter than ever. Arif had filled the box with hundreds of new notes from his own life.

Soon, others began doing the same. They wrote down acts of kindness and shared them with one another. Before long, lanterns appeared outside every home in the village.

Travelers crossing the mountains could see the village glowing from miles away.

People called it the Village of Lights.

But the villagers knew the truth:

The lanterns weren't powered by magic.

They were powered by the simple choice to make someone else's day a little brighter. ✨
